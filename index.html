The Leef Story

Leef was designed in 2022 as a proof of concept and was originally just a very basic web page, not a standalone browser. Around 2023, you would go there instead of google.com. It was popular in a small group of about 30 people, but eventually, it was abandoned.

Development on the full browser started in July 2025, and it was released into public alpha in April 2026. It was made to be a fast and AI-free alternative to Google.

Every browser company is adding AI slop to their browser. I got tired of it, so I made the opposite: a no-AI browser.

News, Sports & Privacy

How we fetch news: Leef Browser uses Yahoo News' RSS feeds. Unlike other browsers that might track what you read, Leef fetches the news directly from Yahoo's servers to your machine. We don't act as a "middleman," so we never see what headlines you're looking at.

"I Don't Care" Button: When you dismiss a headline, that preference is stored exclusively in your local browser settings. We do not send this feedback to Yahoo or any other server. It's just a local filter to help you curate your own feed.

Sports Scores & Trademarks: Live scores are fetched directly from ESPN's public APIs. All sports league names, team names, logos, and trademarks (including FIFA®, NFL®, NBA®, MLB®, NHL®) are registered trademarks of their respective owners. Their use on Leef Browser is solely for informational purposes to identify matches and does not imply any affiliation, sponsorship, or endorsement.

Third-Party Analytics: Because you are fetching the RSS feed and scores directly, Yahoo and ESPN may see your IP address and basic browser info. If you click on an article, you are leaving Leef's local environment, where their standard privacy policies apply.

Affiliation: Leef Browser is an independent project and is not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by Yahoo, ESPN, FIFA®, or any other third-party trademark owners.

Global Privacy Control

What is Global Privacy Control (GPC)?
GPC is a legal browser signal that automatically tells websites you want to opt-out of having your personal data sold or shared. Under laws like California's CCPA and Europe's GDPR, participating websites are legally required to respect this signal.

🛡️

Search Engine Safe Mode

Preventing search engine crashes

Google recently changed its search engine backend to prioritize its "AI Overview" features. Because Leef injects specialized filters to strip out these AI components, the complex scripts served by Google can conflict with our blocker, causing the underlying Chromium layout engine to crash.

To keep your browsing seamless and avoid infinite crash loops, Leef automatically loads your query in Safe Mode using DuckDuckGo.
